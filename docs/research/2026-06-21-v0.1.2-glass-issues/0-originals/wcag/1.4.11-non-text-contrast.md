# WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 1.4.11: Non-text Contrast

**Full Criterion Text:**
"The visual presentation of the following have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 against adjacent color(s): User Interface Components—Visual information required to identify user interface components and states, except for inactive components or where the appearance of the component is determined by the user agent and not modified by the author; Graphical Objects—Parts of graphics required to understand the content, except when a particular presentation of graphics is essential to the information being conveyed."

**Intent:**
The criterion ensures user interface components and meaningful graphics are distinguishable by people with moderately low vision. Low contrast controls may be missed entirely by users with visual impairments. If a graphic is essential to understanding content or functionality, it must be perceivable without contrast-enhancing assistive technology. The 3:1 ratio should be treated as a threshold value—computed values should not be rounded (e.g., 2.999:1 fails). Authors should refer to underlying markup rather than rendered results due to anti-aliasing variations.

**Benefits:**
People with low vision struggle to perceive low-contrast graphics, especially those with color vision deficiencies. A 3:1 relative luminance difference makes items more distinguishable when users cannot see a full color range.

**Examples:**
Status icons on dashboards with 3:1 minimum contrast; text inputs with dark borders around white areas; graphs with light backgrounds where line colors contrast at least 3:1. Pie charts fail when slice edges cannot be discerned at 3:1 contrast. Infographics fail when circles and icons lack sufficient contrast.

**Focus Indicator Requirements:**
Focus indicators must contrast with adjacent backgrounds. Internal indicators must contrast with component backgrounds; border indicators must contrast with colors on both sides; partially内外 indicators can use either portion for contrast. Indicators within component boundaries must contrast with adjacent internal colors. External indicators must contrast with the background the component sits on. Note: Changes in background color alone (without other indicators) may fail Use of Color (1.4.1) rather than Non-text Contrast.
